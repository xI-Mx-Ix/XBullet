> # ✨ XBullet ✨ - Physics Engine for Minecraft 1.20.1 Forge
>
> **XBullet** is an **experimental Physics Engine** for **Minecraft 1.20.1**, based on **Bullet Physics** and specifically the adapted `libbulletjme` implementation. The mod is currently in a **very early Alpha stage**. The goal is to introduce **more realistic physics** into Minecraft, including authentic **collisions** and **physics-based movement** for objects.
>
> > ⚠️ **Important Warning:** This mod is still under **heavy development** (Alpha) and may contain **many bugs** or lead to **unexpected behavior**. It is currently intended **solely for testing purposes**. Please report any **bugs, crashes, or unusual behavior** to help improve the mod!
>
> ## 🎯 Planned Features & Current Status
>
> **A lot is planned** for the future of XBullet! The mod will be expanded step by step.
>
> *   Implementation of a **realistic physics engine** in Minecraft.
> *   Utilizes **Bullet Physics** (specifically the `linbulletjme` port).
> *   Fundamentals for **realistic collisions** and **physical movement**.
> *   Supports the **Minecraft Forge 1.20.1** version.
> *   The **API** for interacting with the physics engine will be made **usable for other developers** in future versions.
>
> ## ➡️ Usage (Test Command)
>
> Currently, a test command serves to demonstrate the basic physics:
>
> With the command:
> ```
> /physicsobject block_obj ~ ~ ~
> ```
> you can spawn a stone block that behaves according to the physics rules, including **realistic collisions** and **movement**.

> ## 📜 Disclaimer & Important Notes
>
> *   This mod is in **Alpha stage** and usage is **at your own risk**.
> *   **All features and changes** are not final and may change significantly in future updates.
> *   **How to delete Physics Objects (currently):** Currently, physics objects cannot be removed normally within the game. To delete them, you must navigate to your Minecraft world folder and delete the file located at `/data/xbullet_PhysicsObjects.dat`. **Note:** This will delete *all* physics objects in the world. A better method will be implemented in the future.
